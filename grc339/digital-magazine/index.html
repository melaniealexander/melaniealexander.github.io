Italy’s Sofia Rafaelli Claims Historic Gold Medals

The opening day of the 2022 World Rhythmic Gymnastics Championships in Sofia, Bulgaria, was - fittingly, given the location - all about Italian Sofia Raffaeli. Earlier in the day, pre-event favourites Daria Atamamov and Boryana Kaleyn withdrew from the competition providing the unexpected opportunity.

Alina Harnasko wins silver at World Cup in Milan

The Belarusian qualified for two finals in separate exercises, most successfully with hoop, received a score of 34.250, ahead of many strong competitors: German Daria Varfolomeev, and Italian Sofia Rafaelli.

Linoy Ashram wins gold in Rhythmic gymnastics individual all-around

Israel’s Linoy Ashram stunned pre-event favourites ROC’s Dina and Arina Averina to win gold in rhythmic gymnastics individual all-around finals today with an overall score 107.800.

Darja Varfolomeev and Germany’s long road to rhythmic gymnastics glory

The 17-year-old won gold in the rhythmic gymnastics individual all-around competition on Friday. Varfolomeev posted the highest score in all four events — hoop, ball, clubs and ribbon — on her way to a total of 142.850, well clear of silver medalist Boryana Kaleyn of Bulgaria and bronze medalist Sofia Raffaeli of Italy.

Golden Success for Bulgarian Girls’ Team at European Rhythmic Gymnastics Cup

Bulgaria claimed the girls’ team title at the European Rhythmic Gymnastics Cup, marking a significant achievement at the tournament held for the first time in Burgas. The team, composed of Magdalena Valkova, Antoaneta Tsankova, and Anastasia Kaleva, accumulated a total of 103.248 points to secure the top position.

Brazil’s Barbara Domingos wins first-ever rhythmic Pan Am Games all-around gold

Domingos’ gold medal is the first rhythmic gymnastics win at the Pan Am Games for Brazil and, together with teammate Alexandre, it’s the first time that Brazil has placed two women on the rhythmic gymnastics all-around podium at the Games.

Inside Gymnastics Perspective: The Mental Side of the Mat

USA Gymnastics has significantly enhanced its mental health support for athletes, including those in rhythmic gymnastics. Initiatives encompass hiring trained psychologists, implementing emergency assessments, and introducing therapy animals like Beacon the golden retriever to provide emotional support during competitions.

Beacon the Golden Retriever is Helping USA Gymnastics with Mental Health

Beacon, a 4-year-old golden retriever, has become an integral part of the USA Gymnastics team, offering pet-assisted therapy to athletes. His presence has been instrumental in alleviating stress and promoting mental well-being among gymnasts, including those in the rhythmic discipline.

Masterclass Spotlight: Viktoriia Onopriienko’s Summer Intensive

Ukrainian rhythmic gymnast Viktoriia Onopriienko is set to lead a three-day masterclass at Freedom Academy NJ this summer. The event aims to provide aspiring gymnasts with high-level training and insights from an elite athlete. Proceeds from the masterclass will support charitable causes, reflecting the community spirit within the sport.

Code of Points 2025–2028: Emphasizing Artistry and Safety

The International Gymnastics Federation (FIG) has released the updated Code of Points for the 2025–2028 Olympic cycle. These revisions strive to balance technical difficulty with artistic expression, ensuring the sport’s evolution aligns with athlete well-being.
